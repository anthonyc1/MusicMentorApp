What are scale conversions?

Scale conversions are ways to change between major and minor scales that relate to one another. Using scales that are related are common in many musical pieces because related scales are compatible and sound good together. Scales are related to each other in two ways: sharing the same key signature or the same tonic note. Often you will see composers writing separate sections of music using these related keys.

What a key?

A key is the central part of any piece of music. For instance, it tells you the number of sharps(#) and flats(b) used, the scale that the piece is based on.

Relative Keys

Relative keys are major and minor scales that have the same key signature. One example is the C Major Scale and the A Minor Scale. Both scales have no sharps(#) or flats(b) in their key signatures.

To convert between them, you need to know which major and minor scales have the same key signature. One way to figure this out is to use the Circle of Fifths, which is a diagram that conveniently displays the relationship between all the major and minor scales.

Parallel Keys

Parallel keys are easier to determine because they are major and minor scales with the same tonic note. The tonic note of a scale is the note the scale is named after. For example, the C Major Scale and the C Minor Scale are parallel keys, because both have the same tonic note.
