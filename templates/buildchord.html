What are chords?

A chord is three or more musical notes played at the same time. A Major chord is constructed from a minor third on top of a major third. In other words, we take the root note C, the scale degree 3 note E, and the scale degree 5 note G. A major third has four half steps while a minor third has three half steps.

In this case, a C Major Chord is just C E G.

A Minor chord is constructed from a major third on top of a minor third. Let's use the F minor chord. In this case the root is F, the scale degree 3 note is A, and the scale degree 5 note is C. If we take into account of the half steps, the F minor chord is actually F Ab C.
