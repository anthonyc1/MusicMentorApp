What are scales?

The best way to think of a scale is a set of different pitches that are grouped together in an ascending order. Major and minor scales usually span an octave, or a series of eight notes where each note is a different pitch.

To visualize, here's a piano keyboard below displaying a C Major Scale:

Note how the first note of the scale is a C note and the last note of the scale is also a C note. See how the C Major Scale is a series of eight notes? They form an octave. The end notes, in this case the two C notes, of an octave are always twice or half the frequency of vibration of the other. In general, notes towards the left on a piano or keyboard are of lower frequency than notes towards the right.

Now let's visualize a different scale. Here's a piano keyboard below displaying a C Minor Scale:

Note how the first note and last note of the scale are also C notes, but wait, something is different. The notes in between are not like the notes in the C Major Scale. That's because minor scales group a different set of different pitches than major scales. One big effect of this different grouping is that it dramatically changes the mood of the music. Music based on major scales tend to sound more happy while music based on minor scales tend to sound serious or sad.
